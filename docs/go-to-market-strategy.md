# Go-To-Market Strategy Framework

## Overview
A repeatable GTM playbook for B2B SaaS companies targeting SMB and mid-market segments. Built from analysis of 3 product launches across different verticals.

## GTM Motion Selection Matrix

| Company Stage | ACV | Primary Motion | Secondary Motion |
|--------------|-----|---------------|------------------|
| Pre-PMF | <$5K | PLG / Founder-led | Content SEO |
| Early traction | $5K-$20K | Inside Sales + PLG | Paid Search |
| Scale | $20K-$80K | Field Sales | ABM + Events |
| Enterprise | >$80K | Enterprise Sales | Partner/Channel |

## Part 1: Market Segmentation

### TAM/SAM/SOM Calculation
```
TAM = Total addressable market (all possible buyers)
SAM = Segment addressable market (buyers you can realistically reach)
SOM = Serviceable obtainable market (realistic 3-year capture)

Example (B2B HR Tech):
- TAM: 500,000 companies with 50-500 employees in EMEA = $2.1B
- SAM: 120,000 companies using HRIS systems = $504M
- SOM: 2,400 companies (2% capture in 3 years) = $10M ARR
```

### Segment Prioritization Score
Score each segment 1-5 on:
- Market size
- Willingness to pay
- Sales cycle length (inverse)
- Competitive intensity (inverse)
- Product-market fit signal

**Target segments scoring 20+ first.**

## Part 2: Positioning

### Positioning Statement Template
```
For [target customer]
Who [has this problem/need]
[Product name] is a [category]
That [primary benefit]
Unlike [main alternative]
We [key differentiator]
```

### Competitive Positioning Map
Plot competitors on 2 axes most relevant to your buyers:
- Axis 1: Ease of implementation vs. Feature depth
- Axis 2: SMB focus vs. Enterprise focus

Find the whitespace where customer pain is highest and competition is lowest.

### Messaging Hierarchy
```
Level 1: Category claim (what you are)
Level 2: Primary benefit (what you do)
Level 3: Proof points (why believe you)
Level 4: Feature specifics (how you do it)
```

## Part 3: Channel Strategy

### Channel-Fit Framework
```
Self-serve / PLG works when:
- ACV < $5,000
- Product is intuitive (time-to-value < 30 min)
- Buyer = end user
- Category is known

Inbound + SDR works when:
- ACV $5K-$30K
- Buyer ≠ user (economic buyer + champion)
- Category needs education
- Sales cycle 30-90 days

ABM + Field Sales works when:
- ACV > $30K
- Multiple stakeholders (5+ personas)
- Long sales cycle (90-180+ days)
- Strategic accounts drive 80% of revenue
```

## Part 4: Sales Motion Design

### MEDDPICC Qualification Framework
- **M**etrics: Quantifiable business impact
- **E**conomic buyer: Who controls the budget
- **D**ecision criteria: How they evaluate vendors
- **D**ecision process: Steps to signed contract
- **P**aper process: Legal, security, procurement
- **I**dentify pain: Root cause problem
- **C**hampion: Internal advocate with power
- **C**ompetition: Who else is in the deal

### Sales Velocity Formula
```
Sales Velocity = (# Opportunities × Win Rate × ACV) / Sales Cycle Length

Example:
- 50 opps × 25% win rate × $24,000 ACV / 60 days = $5,000/day
- Monthly revenue = $150,000
```

### Pipeline Coverage Requirements
```
For $1M ARR target:
- Assume 25% close rate → need $4M in pipeline
- At $25K ACV → need 160 qualified opportunities
- If SDR generates 8 opps/month → need 20 SDRs or 20 months
- Optimize: Increase ACV, improve win rate, or add inbound
```

## Part 5: Launch Playbook

### Pre-Launch (T-90 to T-30)
- [ ] ICP validation: 20 customer interviews completed
- [ ] Positioning tested: Message resonance >70% in interviews
- [ ] Sales enablement: Battlecards, objection handling complete
- [ ] Content: 10 SEO articles targeting bottom-of-funnel terms live
- [ ] Paid: Landing pages A/B tested, CVR >3%
- [ ] CRM: Lead scoring, routing, and nurture sequences built

### Launch (T-30 to T+30)
- [ ] PR/analyst outreach: Target 3 tier-1 publications
- [ ] LinkedIn campaign: Thought leadership + product content
- [ ] Email: Existing contacts and opt-in lists
- [ ] Paid campaigns: Brand + competitor + category terms
- [ ] Partner activation: Co-marketing with 2-3 partners
- [ ] Sales outreach: 500 targeted accounts in Outreach/Salesloft

### Post-Launch (T+30 to T+90)
- [ ] Review CAC by channel (target: CAC payback <18 months)
- [ ] Win/loss analysis: Interview 5 won, 5 lost deals
- [ ] Funnel analysis: Identify top drop-off points
- [ ] ICP refinement: Update scoring based on early customers
- [ ] Channel reallocation: Double down on 2-3 top performers

## Part 6: KPIs and North Star Metrics

### GTM Health Dashboard
```
Acquisition:
- MQL volume and MQL-to-SQL rate (target: >20%)
- CAC by channel (target: <12 month payback)
- Paid efficiency: MQL CPL < 1/12 of ACV

Conversion:
- SQL-to-Opportunity rate (target: >50%)
- Opportunity-to-Close rate (target: >25%)
- Average sales cycle (target: <60 days for SMB)

Retention:
- Net Revenue Retention (target: >110% for healthy SaaS)
- Logo churn (target: <5% annually)
- Time-to-value (target: <7 days for PLG)

Efficiency:
- LTV:CAC ratio (target: >3:1)
- Magic Number (target: >0.75)
- Burn multiple (target: <1.5x)
```
