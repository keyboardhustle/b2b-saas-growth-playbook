## Part 4: Buying Triggers

What happens in a company that causes them to start evaluating your product *right now*?

Triggering events are the most important and most overlooked part of ICP definition. Target companies experiencing these first.

| Trigger | Signal to watch |
|---------|----------------|
| Hired a new [title] | LinkedIn job postings / announcements |
| Raised a funding round | Crunchbase, news |
| Hit a growth milestone | Press release, blog post |
| Had a public product launch | ProductHunt, press |
| Competitor of their competitor launched | Industry news |
| Regulatory change affecting them | Industry news, gov sources |
| Specific intent data signal | G2, Bombora, Clearbit |

## Part 5: Anti-Personas (Who NOT To Target)

Explicitly define who you are *not* selling to. This prevents your sales team from wasting time and your marketing from generating volume without value.

| Anti-Persona Attribute | Reason to Exclude |
|------------------------|-------------------|
| Company size < [X] employees | Too small to afford / too high-touch |
| Industry: [X] | Poor fit with product |
| Geography: [X] | Compliance, language, or support constraints |
| Using tool: [X] | Locked into competitor ecosystem |
| Stage: Pre-seed/idea stage | No budget, no process, no urgency |

## Part 6: ICP Scoring Criteria

Use this to score inbound leads automatically in your CRM. Each criterion = points. Set a threshold (e.g. score > 60 = ICP-fit).

| Criterion | Score |
|-----------|-------|
| Industry matches primary vertical | +20 |
| Company size in target range | +15 |
| Geography in target region | +10 |
| Persona title matches primary buyer | +20 |
| Uses at least 2 matching technographic tools | +10 |
| Triggered by qualifying event (funding, hire, etc.) | +15 |
| Intent signal detected | +10 |
| **Total possible** | **100** |

**Routing rules:**
- Score ≥ 70: Route to AE immediately. High-priority follow-up within 1 hour.
- Score 40–69: Route to SDR for qualification. Follow-up within 24 hours.
- Score < 40: Nurture only. Do not route to Sales.

## Part 7: How to Validate Your ICP

Your ICP is a hypothesis until you validate it. Run these checks:

1. **CRM analysis:** Look at your last 20 Closed Won deals. What do they have in common? Map them to this framework.
2. **Win/loss pattern:** Where does your win rate > 40%? That’s your ICP. Where is it < 20%? That’s your anti-persona.
3. **Revenue concentration:** What % of ARR comes from which segments? Protect and expand in those segments first.
4. **Talk to customers:** 5 customer interviews will tell you more than any spreadsheet. Ask: why did you buy, what nearly stopped you, what would you tell a peer considering us?
5. **Quarterly review:** ICP drifts as your product and market evolve. Review and update this doc every quarter.
